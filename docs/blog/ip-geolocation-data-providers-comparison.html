Detailed Breakdown

1. MaxMind — The Gold Standard

Pros: Industry standard. GeoLite2 is free. GeoIP2 has ~95% city fill. Used by Cloudflare, npm, Cisco.

Cons: GeoIP2 at $160K/yr is for enterprises only. GeoLite2 redistribution requires a separate commercial license (price TBD — awaiting quote). Free GeoLite2 license (CC BY-SA) does NOT cover commercial redistribution through an API.

Verdict: Wait for GeoLite commercial license quote. If <$5K/yr, it's the safest choice — same data we already use, just legally compliant.

2. DB-IP — The Best Alternative

Pros: Commercial DB has better city fill than GeoLite2. Daily updates. MMDB format compatible with our existing code. Pricing likely affordable for startups.

Cons: Commercial pricing not public — need to contact. Company is smaller (Eris Networks S.A.S, France). Less industry recognition than MaxMind.

Verdict: Strongest Plan B. If MaxMind GeoLite commercial is too expensive, DB-IP is the most practical drop-in replacement.

3. IP2Location — Clear Internal Pricing, Murky Redistribution

Pros: Transparent internal pricing: $99/yr/server, $980/yr unlimited site. Long history (since 2001). Has both location and proxy databases. MMDB format supported.

Cons: SaaS/redistribution license price is hidden behind "Contact Us". LITE edition requires attribution (CC BY-SA). DB product lineup is confusing (25+ variants).

Verdict: Worth asking for a redistribution quote. Their internal pricing suggests they're affordable. IP2Proxy LITE could replace our Tor/VPN detection.

4. ipgeolocation.io — API-First, Not DB-First

Pros: Good SDK ecosystem. Has security databases (VPN/proxy detection).

Cons: No downloadable free database. They want you to use their API, not host their data. DB pricing page was 404. "Request Quote" for everything. Feels like they don't want small customers.

Verdict: Skip. Not suitable as a data source for building your own API.
